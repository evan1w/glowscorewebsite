METHODOLOGY

How we score food

No black box. Every score is a deterministic function of ingredients and macros, anchored in published research.

01 · INPUT

Open Food Facts + USDA

2.5M+ products. Ingredient lists, macro/micro panels, serving sizes. Open data, auditable.

Source database →
02 · WEIGHTING

Peer-reviewed weights

Each ingredient and nutrient carries a coefficient set from meta-analyses on satiety, glycemic response, and inflammation.

PubMed references →
03 · OUTPUT

Three normalized scores

Leanness, skin, bloat — each rescaled 0–100 against the full product distribution. Same input always returns the same score.

04 · UPDATES

Monthly re-calibration

New studies trigger coefficient updates. Changelog published in-app so you can see what moved and why.

FAQ

Questions, answered straight

Is this just Yuka with a different coat of paint?
No. Yuka returns one composite "health" score. FoodScanner returns three independent scores tied to outcomes you can feel — leanness, skin, bloat — and lets you share scans with friends so you can compare notes.
Where do the scores actually come from?
A deterministic formula over the ingredient list and macro/micro panel. Coefficients come from meta-analyses on satiety (leanness), dermatology research on diet-acne links (skin), and GI clinical trials on FODMAPs and emulsifiers (bloat). Same input → same score, every time. Methodology section above has source links.
Does it work for restaurant food or just packaged products?
Barcoded packaged food is the strongest case — 2.5M products covered. Restaurant dishes work via menu lookup for chains; one-off independent restaurants are best-effort and we tell you when confidence is low.
Is the AI making stuff up?
Scores are not generated by an LLM. They're a deterministic calculation on ingredients and macros. The plain-English verdict is generated, but it's constrained to cite the actual ingredients and nutrients that drove the score — it can't invent flags that aren't on the label.
What's free vs paid?
Free: 5 scans/day, body impact scores, sharing, 7-day history. Premium ($5/mo annual): unlimited scans, deep micronutrient breakdown, lifetime history.
Do you sell my data?
No. Scan history stays on-device by default; cloud sync is opt-in and encrypted. Zero ad networks, zero analytics SDKs that resell behavior. Privacy policy is short and in plain English.
Android?
iOS first. Android is on the roadmap once iOS retention crosses target. Drop your email in the App Store reviews and we'll see it.
