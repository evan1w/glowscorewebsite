METHODOLOGY

Not vibes. Not AI hallucinations.

Every score is a deterministic calculation from the ingredient list and nutrition panel. Same food always returns the same score.

01 · INPUT

Open Food Facts + USDA

2.5M+ products. Ingredient lists, macro/micro panels, serving sizes. Open data, fully auditable.

02 · WEIGHTING

Peer-reviewed coefficients

Each ingredient and nutrient carries a weight from meta-analyses on satiety, glycemic response, and inflammation. PubMed-sourced.

03 · OUTPUT

Three normalized scores

Each score rescaled 0–100 against the full product distribution. Not relative to your diet — absolute against the universe of scanned foods.

04 · UPDATES

Monthly re-calibration

New studies move coefficients. Changelog published in-app. You can see exactly what changed and why.

FAQ

Questions, answered straight.

Is this just Yuka with a different coat of paint?
No. Yuka gives one composite score. Glowscore gives three scores tied to specific body outcomes — skin, bloat, leanness — and a mascot that reflects your eating habits in real time.
Where do scores actually come from?
A deterministic formula over the ingredient list and macro panel. Coefficients from meta-analyses on satiety, dermatology research on diet-acne links, and GI clinical trials on FODMAPs. Same input → same score, always.
Does it work for restaurant food?
Barcoded packaged food is the strongest case — 2.5M products covered. Restaurant chains work via menu lookup. Independent restaurants are best-effort.
Is the AI making stuff up?
Scores are not LLM-generated. They're a deterministic calculation. The plain-English verdict is AI-generated but constrained to only cite ingredients and nutrients that actually drove the score.
What's free vs paid?
Free: 3 scans/day (unlimited on day 1), last 7 scans saved, 1 alternative suggestion, 1 personalized article, 1 lifetime streak freeze, flag names only (no explanations), score numbers only (no breakdown text). Premium ($5/mo annual): unlimited scans, lifetime history, full score breakdowns, red flag explanations, all personalized articles, unlimited streak freeze protection, Sage Week in Review.
Do you sell my data?
No. No ad networks, no analytics SDKs that resell behavior. Privacy policy is short and plain English.
Android?
iOS first. Android is on the roadmap.
